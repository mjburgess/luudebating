Welcome

We provide an open forum for discussion for members of LUU and the Leeds Community, promoting critical thinking, open discussion, free speech and uncensored debate. Training is available and opportunities for debating and public speaking. LDU have a national and international reputation for excellence.

On Campus

LDU members are offered the chance to become part of a prominent student We work with students and the Leeds University Union and the Leeds Metropolitan Student's Union in order to promote and facilitate free speech on campus . We host and take part in a wide range of activities within the university designed at fostering critical thought and discussion in the university.

Off Campus

LDU members travel the country, representing the society and promoting free speech nationwide. Our speakers compete nationally and are often invited to other institutions to take part in public debates or chair internal competitions. We have a strong national reputation - with many of our members reaching the finals and semi finals of large national competition. See the Achievements sections for a more detailed overview of our national success.

Internationally

We send speakers around the world to represent the University in the European Championships and World Championships. Locations have ranged from Galway, Ireland, to Manilla, The Philippines. We aid our members financially and provide them with the oratorical and analytical skill they'll need in order to take on the world's best. The trips are always the highlight of the year and are a fantastic opportunity to see the world and gain a truly unique experience.

Our Community

We meet every Thursday and Tuesday, where we train members in workshops and seminars then run a debate. Our members are offered training in argumentation, rhetoric and a broad range of critical subjects - they are taught not just how to give an effective argument, but also how to judge one. We debate competitively internally and externally throughout the year and host regular social events for LDU members. Many of our members have written for the Guardian, the Independent, New Statesman and Leeds Student .

Local Community

Our debaters are actively engaged with local affairs and the community around Leeds. We work with local figures and groups in order to foster discussion and build a strong local voice. We are also heavily involved in local schools; our members have the opportunity to volunteer in local schools around Leeds, coaching young students in the art and virtues of debate. We give mentors the training they'll need in order to make a difference and also advise schools on how they can utilise debate in their classrooms.

Wider Community

In 2012 the LDU established a link with 'Springboard4Cambodia'. The Cambodian based charity worked with 3 LDU members in order to promote speech and debate within Cambodian schools. Our members were flown out to Phnom Penh and funded to aid in project aimed at raising aspiration in the country. We hope to continue to be able to offer this opportunity to future LDU members.
